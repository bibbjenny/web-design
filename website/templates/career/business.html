A Business Analyst analyses processes, and recommends solutions to help organisations achieve their goals by bridging the gap between business requirements and IT solutions.

What Does a Business Analyst Do?

A Business Analyst acts as a bridge between business needs and IT solutions, helping organisations improve their processes, products, and services. They work closely with stakeholders to understand business requirements and translate them into detailed technical specifications for IT teams. Business Analysts also identify opportunities for improvement, recommend solutions, and ensure that the final deliverables meet the business objectives.

Why Consider a Career as Business Analyst?

  • Impactful Role: Business Analysts play a key role in shaping business strategy and ensuring successful project outcomes.
  • Diverse Opportunities: They work across various industries, offering flexibility and a wide range of career opportunities.
  • Continuous Learning: The role involves continuous learning and adaptation to new business challenges and technologies.

Key Responsibilities


Requirements Gathering

Working with stakeholders to understand and document business needs and objectives.

Process Analysis

Evaluating existing business processes and identifying areas for improvement or optimisation.

Solution Design

Recommending solutions and working with IT teams to translate business needs into technical specifications.

Stakeholder Communication

Acting as a liaison between business stakeholders and technical teams to ensure alignment.

Skills required:

  • ✔   Strong analytical and problem-solving skills.
  • ✔   Excellent communication and interpersonal abilities.
  • ✔   Ability to translate business needs into technical requirements.
  • ✔   Proficiency with data analysis tools and software.
  • ✔   Understanding of business processes and project management.
