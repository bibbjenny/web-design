Career opportunities > System Administrator

A System Administrator manages and maintains an organisation's computer systems, ensuring they run smoothly, securely, and efficiently.

What Does a System Administrator Do?

A System Administrator (SysAdmin) is responsible for managing and maintaining an organisation’s IT infrastructure, ensuring that computer systems run efficiently and securely. They handle a wide range of tasks, including installing and configuring hardware and software, monitoring system performance, and troubleshooting issues to minimise downtime. System Administrators also implement and enforce security protocols to protect against cyber threats and ensure that data is backed up and recoverable. Their work is crucial in keeping an organisation's technology systems up and running, which allows business operations to proceed smoothly.

Why Consider a Career as System Administrator?

  • Essential Role: System Administrators play a critical role in ensuring the smooth operation of an organisation’s IT systems.
  • Variety of Work: The job offers a variety of tasks and challenges, making it dynamic and engaging.
  • Career Growth: With the increasing reliance on technology, there are many opportunities for career advancement in system administration.

Key Responsibilities


System Maintenance

Installing, configuring, and maintaining servers, networks, and other IT infrastructure.

Performance Monitoring

Continuously monitoring system performance and making adjustments to ensure optimal operation.

Troubleshooting

Identifying and resolving hardware, software, and network issues to minimise disruptions.

Security Management

Implementing security measures, including firewalls, access controls, and data backups, to protect against cyber threats.

Skills required:

  • ✔   In-depth knowledge of operating systems like Windows, Linux, or UNIX
  • ✔   Strong problem-solving skills and the ability to troubleshoot complex technical issues
  • ✔   Familiarity with networking concepts, including LAN/WAN and VPN
  • ✔   Understanding of cybersecurity principles and data protection
